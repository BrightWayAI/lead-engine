# Voice Rules

Hard rules every DM must pass before output. The voice samples in `user-context.md` are the source of truth for *how* the user writes. This file is the source of truth for *what to avoid* and *what structure works*.

---

## The 27-word opener pattern

Four sentences. ~27 words total. Hard cap: 40 words.

1. **Hook** — name the specific signal. Not "saw your post" — "saw your comment about X on Jane's post."
2. **Resonance** — show you got the substance. Not flattery.
3. **Bridge** — connect their thing to yours. No pitch.
4. **Light ask** — low-friction next step.

Why 27 words: LinkedIn DMs cut off at the preview length. Anything longer than ~40 words gets a "see more" expand-prompt, which kills reply rate. Short DMs read like a peer; long DMs read like a sales sequence.

Don't pad. If your DM hits the four beats in 22 words, leave it at 22.

## Banned phrases (always)

These get flagged automatically. Search for them literally before output and rewrite if found:

- "Just checking in"
- "Just wanted to"
- "Circling back"
- "Touching base"
- "Bumping this up"
- "Hope this finds you well"
- "Hope you're doing well"
- "Hope all is well"
- "I hope you're having a great week"
- "Quick question"
- "Quick chat"
- "Pick your brain"
- "Hop on a call"
- "Jump on a call"
- "I'd love to" (overused; replace with "want to" or just the verb)
- "Exciting opportunity"
- "Exciting time at"
- "Game-changing"
- "Game changer"
- "Best in class"
- "Cutting edge"
- "Reaching out because"
- "Reaching out to"
- "I noticed that you"
- "I came across your profile"
- "Saw your impressive"
- "Impressive background"
- "Loved your post"
- "Great post!"
- "Insightful post"
- "Found your insights"
- "Dear [Name]"
- "Hi [Name],"  (in LinkedIn DMs specifically — cold, formal)

Also banned: any sentence that starts with "I" three or more times in a row across consecutive sentences. Vary openings.

---

## Banned shapes (patterns, not just phrases)

- **The compliment-pivot:** "Loved your post! [Pivot to ask]." Compliment is fake; reads predatory.
- **The agenda-revealer:** "I'll be quick — I work at [Co] and we help [Y]." If you have to announce yourself, you've lost.
- **The fake-personalization:** "Hey [Name] — I've been following your work at [Co] for a while now…" If you *had* been following, you'd reference something specific. Skip the lie.
- **The vague compliment:** "Your insights on [topic] really resonated with me." Cite the specific insight or skip the line.
- **The over-promise:** "I have a way to 10x your [metric]." Outside of edge cases, this kills credibility.
- **The double-CTA:** asking twice in one DM. Pick one. The lighter, the better.

## What good looks like

Three example openers (hypothetical):

**Engagement signal:**
> Saw your reply on Jane's checkout-friction post — "the discount-stacking thing" point especially. We see that with mid-market DTC clients constantly. We did a teardown for [client] on it last month — want me to send the writeup?

(38 words, 4 sentences, specific reference, low-friction ask, value-first.)

**Job change signal:**
> Saw the move to Acme as Head of RevOps. First-90-day gnarliness in that seat is usually the lead-routing logic, not the stack itself. We've helped 3 RevOps leads rewrite theirs — want a 5-min loom of how we do it?

(40 words. Specific role, specific pain point, specific proof, specific ask.)

**Direct intent signal:**
> Your thread asking about CRO partners — most of the recs you got are full-service agencies. If you want a teardown-only motion (no retainer), we run that. Happy to drop a list of red flags to watch for either way. Want it?

(40 words. Answers the question first. Soft pitch. Useful even if they say no.)

## Tone matching (per user-context tone setting)

The user picks one default tone in setup. Apply it consistently:

### warm-professional
- Sentences average 12–16 words.
- Light contractions ("we're", "you've").
- One piece of warmth per DM (not gushing).
- Example shape: "Saw [signal]. [Resonance]. [Bridge — confident, not pushy.] [Ask — friendly.]"

### casual-peer
- Sentences average 8–14 words.
- Heavy contractions ("you're", "we've", "don't").
- Lower-case openings okay if voice samples show it.
- Example shape: short, dashes for asides, occasional sentence fragments. "saw your take on X. fair point — we ran into it last quarter. wrote a thing on it. send?"

### direct-operator
- Sentences average 6–10 words.
- Blunt. No softeners ("wanted to", "thought I'd"). Cut to the point.
- Example shape: "Saw [signal]. [Direct take]. [Direct offer]. Want it?"

### formal-executive
- Sentences average 14–20 words.
- No contractions. Proper capitalization.
- Conservative. No jokes, no asides.
- Example shape: full sentences, traditional grammar, but still references the specific signal in sentence one. "I noticed your firm's recent announcement of [signal]. [Substantive response.] [Offer framed as professional courtesy.]"

## Voice-sample matching

When `user-context.md` contains voice samples, treat them as the gold standard:

- Match average sentence length within ±20%.
- Match contraction frequency.
- Match punctuation habits (does the user use em-dashes? semicolons? sentence fragments? lowercase starts?).
- Borrow phrases the user uses repeatedly *only* when they fit organically — never shoehorn.

If the voice samples and the chosen tone disagree, voice samples win. They're observed reality; the tone setting is a stated preference.

---

## Length caps by touch

- **Touch 1 (opener):** 27 words target, 40 hard cap.
- **Touch 2 (value-add follow-up):** 35–60 words. Slightly longer because you're *delivering* something (a link, a stat, an insight). The added length is the value, not filler.
- **Touch 3 (breakup):** 25–40 words. Short, polite, door-open.

---

## The light-ask menu

Replace "30-min call" / "quick chat" / "hop on a call" with one of these by default:

- "Want me to send the [teardown / writeup / template / list]?"
- "Worth a 5-min thread on this?"
- "Useful if I [drop you the framework / share the case study / send the loom]?"
- "Reply 'yes' and I'll send it over."
- "Want it?"
- "Open to a 10-min look at [specific thing]?" (only if the signal is hot — direct intent)

Calls are higher friction than the contact's brain registers. Ladder *up* to a call. Lead with an artifact.

---

## Self-check before output

Re-read your draft and ask:

1. Did sentence 1 reference the *specific* signal? (If no, rewrite.)
2. Could a competitor copy-paste this DM and have it land for them? (If yes, too generic — add signal-specific texture.)
3. Did I use any banned phrase? (Search literal strings.)
4. Does it sound like the voice samples in `user-context.md`? (If it sounds like ChatGPT, rewrite shorter and blunter.)
5. Is the ask the lightest possible step the contact can say yes to?

Only ship the DM when all five pass.
